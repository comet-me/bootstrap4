Question

8. When there's a motion before the timeout is called, do we include the motion? .

Answer

This answer also depends on the situation. If the player is lined up, he goes in motion and lastly a timeout is called. We would tag the motion. But, if a player is moving because they were in the incorrect spot, we would not tag that as a motion. Motion is an intended move by the offense. Being in the incorrect area of the field is a player mistake, so no motion. You will often see the Coach trying to instruct the player to move in these cases.

Question

9. Fake FG tagging perspective according to the Whiteboard is Team 1. However, when Team 1 is on defense, there is no Fake FG Block tag

Answer

Fake FG is going to be the Play Type. This is what the team with the ball is performing. The Result for a Fake FG could be Complete, Incomplete, Interception, Rush, Fumble, Penalty, Sack or TDs. There is no Fake FG Block, because the Defense is not attempting to block a FG. Because the team with the ball is not attempting to kick a FG.

Question

10. Punt, penalty flag thrown while ball was still in the air. Ball was caught by the opponent and was tackled at the -11 yardline. Next play, change of possession and started at -11 yardline, no yardage movement. It appears that penalty was not recognized in the game but the system tagged this as a penalty.

Answer

This is a great catch by the team. In this case, I would tag Return and leave a note about a Penalty on the play. The team receiving the punt was called for a penalty. The team punting the ball likely had an option to move up +5 yards and punt the ball again or decline the penalty (they do not want to enforce the penalty) to keep the ball where they opponent was tackled. In our session we talked about some of the random rules for American Football, this is a good example of one. I updated the master copy of the game for future analysts.

Question

11. Which motion direction do we consider in scenarios wherein a player moves to the one direction then later changes to the opposite direction. Like a player moves in motion to the Left then moves back to the right.

Answer

We will tag the direction of the second motion (or last) motion. This will include a player moving more than one time and multiple athletes moving. If multiple athletes move, we will tag the last player in motion.

Question

12. While tagging formation, we've noticed that some clips are Kicking plays, like extra point, FG or punt. Should we skip tagging formation for "K" events? And if so, what would be the accurate note for it using the "Problem Report" button.

Answer

Plays labeled with “K” will automatically be removed from the Formation tagging. They should not be appearing when Formation is tagged.

Question

17. Ball was not caught by the Quarterback after snap - what is the correct tag? System tag it as pass-sack but in previous games this was tag as run-rush play

Answer

If the ball is not caught, we would tag Run-Rush. We are looking for the QB intentions. If he does catch the ball and is attempting to Pass, we would tag play type: Pass and result: Sack.

Question

18. Do we need to tag Yardline during extra point attempts?

Answer

Yes, we will tag Yardline. They normally lineup on the 3 Yardline, unless a penalty occurred.

Question

19. Which Yardline do we choose when LOS is in between 2 yardlines, not in the exact Yardline. Example, LOS is between 44 and 45.

Answer

We will choose the yardline the ball is closest to. If we cannot tell, we will tag the lower yardline. For this example, I would tag 44.

Question

20. Motion versus Formation shifts - According to online definition, formation shift - A team may shift any number of players into new positions, so long as they all come to a complete stop for a full second before the ball is snapped to start the play. Do we follow this definition and if so, there will be no motion?

Answer

In these cases, we will tag the formation that occurs right before the ball is snapped (the 2nd formation). The player in motion would need to stop before the ball is snapped. We would still tag motion in these cases.

Question

21. Should we tag a GN/LS in play result - interception TD,Fumble def TD?also TD from Punt/Punt Rec? The system doesn't seem to auto populate the GN/L column in these scenarios. Do we need to manually compute and enter data in these instances?

Answer

"Interception, Defensive TD - Enter the GN/LS as “0” and include the following in the Notes column. “There was a Defensive Touchdown on this play.” The note will allow your team to bypass the validation errors. Fumble, Defensive TD – Manually calculate the difference between where the ball was picked up and where the play starts. Example: The play starts on the -20 Yard line. QB fumbles the ball and it is picked up by the Defense on the -15. We would calculate a -5 loss on the play. Include the following in the Notes column. “There was a Defensive Touchdown on this play.” The note will allow your team to bypass any validation errors. TD (during a Punt or KO) - Enter the GN/LS as “0” and include the following in the Notes column. “There was a Return for a Touchdown on this play.” The note will allow your team to bypass the validation errors."

Complete

PRO TIP:
If isn't a forward pass, the pass is to the side or behind the QB it cannot be longer called "Pass Complete". The play type will be Run .
If the pass was not thrown even it is a forward pass, we will not tag as pass but rather we will tag as run.

Fumble

A Fumble occurs when a member of the offensive team drops the football, and the defensive team picks up the ball to gain possession. The defense may then advance the ball.

If the offensive team recovers the dropped ball, do not credit it as a fumble.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
All Play Types

PRO TIP:
See the hands of the referee, usually parang hahawi-hawi muna yan sa ulap and then ituturo niya yung direction ng end zone dun sa team nakapick ng ball.

Complete, TouchDown

This result is only for passing plays
A Complete, Touchdown occurs when the quarterback on the offensive team throws a pass to the wide receiver. The ball is caught in the end zone or ran into the end zone.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Pass, Fake Punt and Fake FG

Fumble, Defensive Touchdown

A Fumble, Defensive Touchdown occurs when the offense unintentionally fumbles the football, and the ball is picked up by the defensive team, who then runs the football into the end zone for a touchdown.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
All Play Types

incomplete

An Incomplete This result is only used on passing plays. An Incomplete result occurs when a member of the offensive team passes the football forward to another member of the offense, and the receiver does not catch the ball.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
All Play Types

interception

An Interception occurs when the team on defense catches the pass that was intended for an offensive player. The defense will then be able to return an interception until they are tackled or go out of bounds, and they will take over possession of the football at that spot.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Pass, Fake Punt, Fake FG

PRO TIP:
There is a thinnest line the difference between intercept and fumble. Intercept must be directly catch the ball from whom who thrown it. If by chance the receiver lose control of the ball after a catch then opponent team catch it we don't call it "intercept", but rather we will call it Fumble

interception, Def TD

An Interception, Defensive Touchdown occurs when the defensive team catches the football intended for the offense and runs the ball into their end zone for a touchdown.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Pass, Fake Punt and Fake FG

Penalty

A Penalty occurs when there is a violation of the rules. Which is indicated by a flag and referee hand signals.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
All Play Types

Sack

A Sack occurs when the quarterback is tackled behind the line of scrimmage before he can throw or pass the football.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Pass, Fake FG and Fake Punt

PRO TIPS:

There's a thinnist difference between incomplete and sack, it cannot be called sack if the ball was thrown even in a very short distance.

Sack, Fumble

Sack, Fumble occurs when the offensive quarterback is tackled, loses control of the football and the defense recovers the ball to gain possession.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Pass, Fake FG and Fake Punt

Sack, Fumble Def TD

Sack, Fumble Def. TD occurs when the quarterback attempts to throw the ball but gets tackled behind the line of scrimmage and loses control of the ball. The defensive player picks up the ball and runs it into the end zone for a touchdown.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Pass, Fake FG and Fake Punt

Sack, Safety

A Sack Safety occurs when the quarterback gets tackled inside his end zone while looking to pass. The defensive team will gain 2 points when they tackle the offense for a safety.
Remember, the referee will raise their hands over their head together to signal a safety.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Pass and Fake Punt

Scramble

A Scramble occurs when a quarterback is trying to throw the ball but ends up running the ball either because of defensive pressure or no wide receivers being open.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Pass, Fake FG and Fake Punt

PRO TIPS:

The play type will always be pass, and the result will be a scramble. View the knowledge base for more of the explanation.

Timeout

A Timeout is a temporary pause in the game. Each team gets three timeouts per half. A timeout can be called by the coach, quarterback or a linebacker. A referee will make the signal below and point in the direction of the team that called the timeout.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
All Play Types

Rush

A Rush occurs any time a player runs the football for positive or negative yards. A Rush does not occur if the ball is passed forward.

There is a slight difference between rush and sack, although sack can only be applied on behind the scrimmage. QB on sack attempt to pass the ball but already tackled while run rush QB is not attempting to pass the ball.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Run, Fake FG and Fake Punt

PRO TIP:
At this stage, if the defensive team picks up the ball it cannot be longer called RUSH.
The play type will be Fumble . Which can also lead to Fumble, Def TD if the defensive team can reach to end zone.

Run, not pass

Don't get confused, some run might seem "pass".

Rush, Safety

A Rush, Safety, occurs when the offensive ball carrier is tackled in his own end zone. The defensive team will gain 2 points when they tackle the offensive team for a safety.

The referee will make a hand signal by holding both hands together over their head to signal a "Safety".

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Run and Fake Punt

PRO TIP:
Watch the referee's hand signals to determine a safety.

Rush, TD

A Rush, Touchdown occurs when an offensive player runs the ball into the end zone and scores a touchdown.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Run, Fake FG and Fake Punt

Return

A Return occurs when a player from the defensive team catches a kicked ball from The offensive team and runs the ball down the field toward the end zone.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
KO, KO Rec, Onside Kick, Punt, Punt Rec, FG* and FG Block*

PRO TIP:
not available this time.

Touchback

A Touchback occurs when the football is kicked through the end zone or the ball is down in the end zone.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
KO, KO Rec, Punt and Punt Rec

PRO TIP:

  • The team receiving the kicked ball will get possession of the ball at their -25 yard line.
  • The referee’s hand will point to the end zone direction from offense team

Fair Catch

A Fair Catch occurs when a member of the team that is receiving the kicked ball signals for a fair catch. Once the player signals for the fair catch, the play is over. The team that kicks the ball is not allowed to touch the person making the fair catch.

Note: The player making the fair catch has to catch the ball. If he drops the ball, the opposing team may pick it up and regain possession

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Punt, Punt Rec (sometimes KO and KO Rec)

PRO TIP:
If the camera misses where the ball comes down, a good indication of a fair catch is if there is no tackle and nothing happens when the receiving team catches the ball.

Out of Bounds

This result is for kicking plays only. Out of Bounds occurs when the ball lands outside the white sidelines.

This will not apply to a FG or FG Block Play Type.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Punt, Punt Rec, KO, KO Rec and Onside Kick

PRO TIP:
See the hands of the referee, animoy hinahawi ang mga ulap sa kalangitan.

Downed

This result is only for punt plays. Downed occurs when a punt lands on the ground and the kicking team picks up or stops the ball before the team that is receiving the punt. The kicking team will try to down the ball as close to the opponent's end zone as possible.

Possible Corresponding Play Types:
Punt and Punt Rec

2 Point Conversion

After a touchdown is scored, the offensive team has an opportunity to either try for one or two points.

  • Trying for one point is known as an extra point, and they will attempt to kick the ball through the goal post.
  • Trying for two points is known as a 2 Point Conversion. They can either run or pass the ball as they attempt to get into the end zone.

The result will be “good” if the attempt kick is successful, otherwise the result will be “no good”.
